Data Visualization 2

This data visualzation shows the global Covid-19 situation in 2020.

Global Deaths Rate Map

This map shows the Death Rate (deaths cases per 100 confirmed cases) with each country in 2020. The deaths rate in Yemem is heightest. On average, there are nearly 29 deaths cases for every 100 confirmed cases. Followed by the United Kingdom and Belgium, the death rate is 15.19% and 14.79% respectively. The fourth and fifth Countries are Italy with a death rate of 14.26% and France with a deaths rate of 13.71%. This means 1 to 2 deaths out of every 10 confirmed cases, the situation of Covid-19 is serious in these 5 countries.

Recovered Rate

These 2 bar charts shows the recovered Rate (recovered cases per 100 confirmed cases) in 2020. The recovered rate of Covid-19 in the Western Pacific region is the highest, 77 out of every 100 confirmed cases have been recovered. Followed by Europe and South East Asia, reaching 69% and 67%.

The recovered rate in the top 10 countries with recovery rate exceeds 95%. Among them, the first, second, and third countries are Djibouti, Iceland and Brunei. At the same time, no country in the South-east Asia and Americas has entered the top 10.

Deaths and Recovered with Confirmed Cases

For most countries, with the increase in the number of confirmed cases, the number of deaths and recoveries have increased proportionally.

Among these countries, the highest number of confirmed cases is the United States, with 4,290,259 confirmed cases, 148,011 deaths, and 1,325,804 recovered. In the UK, the number of confirmed cases is 301,708, 45,844 people have died, and only 1,437 people have recovered. On the contrary, the number of confirmed cases in Singapore was 50,838, the number of recovered cases was 45,692, and only 27 people died.

New Confirmed Cases until July 27th, 2020

On the whole, starting from March 8th, the number of daily new cases increased significantly until July 27th. Before March 8th, only the Pacific region had a certain number of confirmed cases. On February 13rd, the number of confirmed cases reached 13,643. After that, the number of new cases began to decrease. Covid-19 was initially controlled. Between March 08th and April 19th, the Europe has a significant increase in new cases. After that, new cases were decreased. On April 12nd, the number of new cases in Europe reached a peak of 56,494 cases. After April 19th, the number of new cases in the Americas has been ahead of other regions, until July 27th. On July 22nd, the number of new cases reached a peak, and 172,649 people were diagnosed that day.
